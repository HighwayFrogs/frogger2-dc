Dreamcast Software Development Standards/Ver. 2.00E

14. Ending/Credit Scroll

14.1 Operation at Ending/Credit Scroll

Required: In case of displaying library logos (such as True Motion and CRI MPEG Sofdec) during ending/credit scrolling, it should not be possible to skip forward. Also, follow the rules in the contract document regarding skipping screens when displaying license logos.
When library logo and/or license logo display is not included in the Ending/Credit scroll display, the application side can decide whether or not to allow screen skipping. Does this work.

However, even if license logo display is not included in the Ending/Credit scroll, enable "Soft Reset" during the Ending/Credit scroll, as mentioned in 4.1.1 Soft Reset Timing.

14.2 Advancing to the Next Screen

Required: After Ending/Credit Scroll finishes, finally advance to the Title Screen with the timer or optional buttons.

14.3 Rules for Credit Scrolls

Required: (SEGA Brand only) Follow the Rules for Credit Scroll outlined in 14.3.1-14.3.4.
Recommended: (Sega Brand Only) Observe the rules of 14.3.5 to 14.3.9.

14.3.1 Credit Scroll Contents Approval

Attach detailed specs for the contents of the credit scroll and bring it to the Legal Affairs Department. Obtain the approval of the director in charge of the creation department.

All titles for SEGA Brand must obtain approval in this way. Be sure to follow this procedure.

14.3.2 Software Copyright Display

The copyright is displayed last in the credit scroll. It appears within brackets [] as shown below.
[PresentedbySEGA]
[(C)SEGAENTERPRISES,LTD.,(Year of first release)]
- "" indicates a space.
- Display the copyright last in the credit scroll.
Å@(After displaying the above, the SEGA Logo may be displayed.)
- If there are two copyrights, display them both within brackets [].
- Position the "Presented.." above the "(C)...".
- Display the same (C)... as the one used in 6.2.

14.3.3 Getting Permission from an Individual to be included in the Credit Scroll
Obtain the permission of the individuals, regardless of whether their real or pen name is to be used.
If permission of 14.3.1 is granted, the name of OEM staff and part-time workers may appear in the credit scroll.
We are currently reviewing the guidelines, but from now on, it may be necessary to have a pledge document submitted by the company for all employees.

14.3.4 Prohibited Items to Mention 1
The following cannot be mentioned in a copyright.
- Words related to copyrights may not appear in personal titles. (COPYRIGHT(C), REGISTERED(R), ORIGINATE, LICENSE, PRESENTS, PRESENTED BY, PRODUCED BY, TM, Production/Writing, etc.)
- Confidential items to be kept secure from other companies such as the development period, development materials, etc.
- Sega internal department names (project team names are acceptable)
- Personal identification materials such as photos and realistic portraits
- Personal identification information such as addresses and telephone numbers
- Unless specifically regulated by contract documents, the original credits cannot be used when transferred to another system.

14.3.5 Prohibited Items to Mention 2

To avoid complications concerning rights, the Legal Affairs Department has issued the following suggestions of items that should not be mentioned in the credit scroll. If a prohibited item must be mentioned in the credit scroll, be sure to confirm its approval when obtaining approval with 14.3.1.
- Including "Scenario", "Words", "Composition" in title names.
- Mentioning a personal name in the credits together with the name of the OEM company or company that provided materials.
- Mentioning an OEM company logo (or company that provided materials) in the credit scroll.

14.3.6 Real/Pen Name Consistency
Staff names should be consistently either real names or pen names as far as possible, to avoid confusion.

14.3.7 Character Set Consistency
Character usage (font/language/character set) should be as consistent as possible.

14.3.8 Title Consistency
Staff titles in the credits should follow actual tasks and avoid wide diversions from actual status or non-sensical titles.

14.3.9 Note on Pen Name Usage
Pen names should follow the general guidelines and proper customs.
