Dreamcast Software Development Standards/Ver. 2.00EU

27. Image and Animation Restrictions (Pokemon Animation Problem)

Required: Animation functions such as rotation should not be done with the Dreamcast logo other than those performed by the Boot ROM.

Required: (SEGA Brand only) Create software while referring to the NHK (Japan Broadcasting Co.) and Japan Civil Broadcasting Union Guidelines.

Execute a check with the SEGA inspection tool (special order product) developed along the guidelines stipulated in the NHK (Japan Broadcasting Co.) and Japan Civil Broadcasting Union Guidelines. If a troublesome condition is detected during check by the tool, (SEGA Brand only), the software must be corrected.

Considering the effect on people prone to light sensitive seizures, (SEGA Brand only) applications cannot be released without revision if a troublesome condition is detected.

Recommended: Applications that contain problem areas as set forth in the guidelines should follow the preventive measures below.

- Prepare an option that can toggle light ON/OFF when flashing lights appear.

- Display a warning message like the one below at the beginning of a game.

"This game uses strobe and flash effects on the screen during play. Viewing this game may cause temporary muscle convulsions or loss of consciousness in people who are sensitive to strong light rays and flashing lights, so please read the instruction manual before playing this game."

We would like to not use the expressions explained in the Guidelines, but it should be included in all cases, as it is assumed not all applications can be checked for problems by the inspection tool.

Note: Inquire to the SEGA Development and Planning section for information about the inspection method, contents and inspection tool.

Note: For 3rd party titles, conforming to the guidelines mentioned above should be done by each 3rd party company. We hope that each company takes responsibility to consider the NHK (Japan Broadcasting Co.) and Japan Civil Broadcasting Union Guidelines.

Note: Also, if a troublesome condition is detected when checking 3rd party titles with SEGA's inspection tool, SEGA will contact the company, but, ultimately, the decision of release and revision lies with each 3rd party company.


27.1 NHK (Japan Broadcasting Co.) and Japan Civil Broadcasting Union Guidelines

1. The rate of blinking of an image or light generally should not exceed three times per second, in addition to the following points:

(1) Bright red colors must be handled especially carefully.

(2) Further to condition 1, if a faster blink rate is necessary it should not exceed five times per second, and in this case, the change in screen brightness should be no more than 20% and total blink time should be less than two seconds.

2. Rolling of high contrast screens or sudden screen changes of more than 20% brightness should generally be limited to no more than three times per second.

3. Avoid allowing a regular pattern (stripes, spirals, concentric circles, etc.) to occupy most of the screen.
